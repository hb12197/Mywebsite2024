Manchester City Football Club (or simply Man City F.C.) is a professional football club based in Manchester, England, that competes in the Premier League, the top flight of English football. Founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton), they became Ardwick Association Football Club in 1887 and Manchester City in 1894. The club's home ground is the City of Manchester Stadium in east Manchester, to which they moved in 2003, having played at Maine Road since 1923. Manchester City adopted their sky blue home shirts in 1894, the first season with the current name.[4] Over the course of its history, the club has won ten league titles, seven FA Cups, eight League Cups, seven FA Community Shields, one UEFA Champions League, one European Cup Winners' Cup, one UEFA Super Cup and one FIFA Club World Cup. Early History (1880–1920s)

Founded as St. Mark's: The club was originally founded in 1880 as St. Mark's in the district of Gorton, Manchester, by a group of churchmen. In 1892, it became Ardwick AFC before changing to Manchester City 1894.
First Successes: After a few years of struggle, Manchester City found success in the early 1900s. They won the Football League Division 1 title in 1904 and their first FA Cup in 1904. This period marked the beginning of the club’s rise in English football.
Decline and Rebuilding: The club's fortunes fluctuated throughout the early decades, with brief periods of success followed by financial struggles. They were relegated to the Second Division in 1938 but returned to the top flight shortly afterward.

Mid-20th Century (1930s–1960s)

Post-War Success: After World War II, City began to rebuild, and in 1956, they won the FA Cup, beating Birmingham City 3-1 in the final.
1960s Golden Age: Manchester City enjoyed its most successful era of the 1960s under manager Joe Mercer. They won:

  • 1968 First Division Title (League Championship)
  • 1969 FA Cup
  • 1970 League Cup
  • 1970 Cup Winners' Cup (first European trophy)
  • 1970 Charity Shield

Early 2000s: Rebuilding and New Ownership

Return to the Premier League: Manchester City bounced back quickly, winning promotion back to the Premier League in 2000 and steadily rebuilding. During this time, the club had several managers, with Kevin Keegan providing some success, guiding the team to a high mid-table finish and briefly competing for European spots in the early 2000s.

The Modern Era (2008–Present)

2009–2010: The arrival of high-profile players such as Robinho and the hiring of manager Mark Hughes signaled the club's ambitions. However, it wasn't until the appointment of Roberto Mancini in 2009 that the club began to taste real success.

Legacy and Influence

Manchester City’s rise to prominence has reshaped English football, both on the pitch and off it. The club’s success has been driven by investment in world-class players and infrastructure, and their influence extends beyond the football field. The club’s football philosophy under Pep Guardiola, based on intricate passing and possession, has been widely praised and has set a new standard in modern football.

The Etihad Stadium remains the club’s home, and the club’s global presence continues to grow, with a vast fanbase worldwide. Through a combination of rich history, strong ownership, and successful management, Manchester City has solidified itself as one of the world’s premier football clubs.
